Trip Highlights

  • The magnificent views of the world's highest peak, Mt. Everest (8,848.68m)
  • World's highest airport at Syangboche (3,780m)
  • Explore a wide range of flora and fauna at Sagarmatha National Park
  • Wildlife like musk deer, colorful pheasants, snow leopards, and Himalayan Tahrs
  • Chance to explore the culture and lifestyles of the local Sherpa people
  • Prayer wheels, colorful flags, Mani stones, and high suspension bridges
  • Visit an ancient monastery in Tengboche
  • Highest glacier on Earth – Khumbu Glacier (4,900m)
  • Amazing panoramic views from Kala Patthar (5,555m)
  • Views of other peaks such as Mt. Lhotse, Cho Oyu, and Mt. Makalu

Trip Overview

Experience the ultimate convenience on your Everest Base Camp trek with our carefully curated itinerary, offering an EBC trek with helicopter return option.

Designed for those seeking to optimize their time and avoid the arduous hike back to Lukla, this perfect plan allows you to relish the beauty of the popular Everest Base Camp trail on foot and then conclude your adventure with a thrilling helicopter flight from Gorakshep to Lukla.

Prepare for an unforgettable journey as you soar high above the Himalayan wonders, making your EBC trek truly extraordinary.

Everest base camp trek with helicopter return introduces you to the world's highest peak as well as the culture and traditions of Nepal. This 14 day trek to Everest Base Camp will take you to a height of roughly 5,364 meters.

It provides panoramic views of many 8,000-meter-high summits. You can witness the incredible view of Pumori, Lhotse, Nuptse, Labuche as well as the iconic Mt. Everest itself. The Everest base camp trek with helicopter return begins and ends in Lukla. The journey starts along the Dudh Koshi River's bank, passing through the Sagarmatha National Park on the way to Namche Bazaar. Namche Bazaar is the main trading point in the Everest region and is known as an acclimatization destination.

Day by Day Itinerary

Welcome to Nepal! Upon arrival at Tribhuvan International Airport, our representative will meet you and transfer you to your hotel. In the evening, enjoy a welcome dinner and trip briefing with your guide.

Airport Pickup Hotel Check-in Welcome Dinner

Explore Kathmandu's UNESCO World Heritage Sites including Swayambhunath (Monkey Temple), Pashupatinath Temple, and Boudhanath Stupa. In the afternoon, complete trek preparations and equipment check.

UNESCO Sites Gear Shopping Permit Collection

Early morning scenic flight to Lukla (2,840m), one of the world's most exciting airports. Begin your trek with a gentle descent to Phakding through beautiful pine and rhododendron forests.

Scenic Flight 3-4 Hours 8km

Cross several suspension bridges over the Dudh Koshi River. The trail climbs steadily with the first views of Everest appearing. Enter Sagarmatha National Park and reach Namche Bazaar, the bustling Sherpa capital.

5-6 Hours 10km First Everest View

Frequently Asked Questions

The EBC trek is rated as moderate to challenging. You don't need technical climbing skills, but good physical fitness is essential. The trail involves 5-7 hours of walking daily at high altitudes.

The best seasons are Spring (March-May) and Autumn (September-November). These months offer stable weather, clear skies, and moderate temperatures ideal for trekking.

Yes, comprehensive travel insurance is mandatory. It must cover high-altitude trekking up to 6,000m and include emergency evacuation by helicopter.

We recommend 2-3 months of preparation including cardio exercises, hiking with a backpack, and leg strengthening. Walking 10km daily is excellent preparation.

The helicopter flies from Gorakshep to Lukla, offering spectacular aerial views of the Himalayas. The flight takes about 20-25 minutes and saves 3-4 days of hiking back.
